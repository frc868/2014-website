CHS TechHOUNDS Robotics Team Launches Women in Technology Workshop

Encouraging girls grades 6-8 to explore interests in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) through an innovative design curriculum and inspiring speakers.

18% of engineers. 19% of computer scientists. 14.6% of executive officers. Women are abysmally underrepresented in technology fields today. Why? Society has created a notion that jobs in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics are “male professions”.

We are here to change that.

On September 26th, 2015, the Carmel High School TechHOUNDS Robotics Team will be launching the Women in Technology Workshop, an all day event to expose girls in grades 6-8 to a variety of technology-focused classes and seminars. Attendees will be given the opportunity to work with other women passionate about STEM in a supportive environment. The program will feature a coding session led by the 1150 Coding Academy, a computer-aided design (CAD) session led by current female engineering high school students and teachers, and several other team-building and entrepreneurship activities.

At the conclusion of the program, we will hosting an interdisciplinary panel featuring women in STEM careers or actively involved in promoting STEM education to discuss how women can achieve success in technology-oriented fields. [Open to both parents and students].

Email techhounds868@gmail.com or go to www.techhounds.com for more information. Those interested in sponsoring the non-profit (tax-deductible) Women in Technology Workshop should email techhounds868@gmail.com (T-shirt and other media recognition available).
